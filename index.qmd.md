title: "What is an Ecosystem and Socioeconomic Profile?"
---

::: {.lead}
[cite_start]Ecosystem and Socioeconomic Profiles (ESP) aim to address the critical need for more pathways to bring essential socio-ecological information into fisheries science and management[cite: 3].
:::

[cite_start]ESPs are both a process and a product[cite: 4]. [cite_start]The ESP process creates a traceable pathway for identifying and integrating key socio-ecological indicators into species-specific assessment and/or management decision making[cite: 4]. [cite_start]The end result of the process is a succinct, focused report that distills information from a variety of sources and makes it more accessible, interpretable, and actionable for scientists and decision makers[cite: 5].

## Background on the Issue
[cite_start]Ecosystem-based science is a primary component of effective marine conservation and resource management, and NOAA Fisheries is committed to ecosystem-based management as our most powerful tool to maintain productive and resilient fisheries[cite: 7]. [cite_start]This approach considers the entire ecosystem, including humans, to balance tradeoffs between ecological, social, and economic needs[cite: 8]. 

[cite_start]Our national standard guidelines require the consideration of ecosystem and socioeconomic processes with regard to specifying optimum yield[cite: 9]. [cite_start]While progress has been made via comprehensive ecosystem status reports, a gap remains between conducting ecosystem research and integrating it within the fishery management process[cite: 10, 11]. [cite_start]Primary obstacles include a lack of a consistent approach to deciding *when* and *how* to test and incorporate this information[cite: 12].

## History of ESPs
[cite_start]The ESP was first conceived in 2014, building on NOAA Fisheries’ rich history of identifying ecosystem and socioeconomic pressures since the 1990s[cite: 15]. 

* [cite_start]**2017:** The first ESP case study using Alaska sablefish was completed[cite: 16].
* [cite_start]**2018–Present:** Developed and refined through the North Pacific Fisheries Management Council review process and recommendations from the Next Generation Stock Assessment Improvement Plan[cite: 16].
* [cite_start]**Current:** ESPs are now being adapted and adopted across the nation with the help of a National ESP Coordination Team[cite: 17].
