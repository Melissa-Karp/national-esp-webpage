---
title: "The 4 Steps of an ESP"
---

[cite_start]The ESP integrates ecosystem and socioeconomic factors into the stock assessment process through a four-step framework: **Focus, Synthesis, Analyze, and Communicate**[cite: 30].

![The Ecosystem and Socioeconomic Profile four-step process. [cite_start]Credit: NOAA Fisheries](../images/step-diagram.png)

### Step 1: Focus on Ecosystem and Socioeconomic Priorities
[cite_start]The first step is to review information from national initiatives and regional priorities to determine if a fish stock is a priority for producing an ESP[cite: 45]. [cite_start]This is done by evaluating available data and vulnerable life history attributes with respect to other stocks in the area[cite: 46]. Tools used include:
* [cite_start]Climate Vulnerability Assessments [cite: 48]
* [cite_start]Stock Assessment Classification and Prioritization [cite: 49]
* [cite_start]Productivity-Susceptibility Assessments [cite: 50]

### Step 2: Synthesize Ecosystem and Socioeconomic Vulnerabilities
[cite_start]Identify vulnerabilities to overfishing, ecological pressures, and climate change throughout the life stages of the stock[cite: 52]. [cite_start]We conduct a thorough literature review and develop conceptual models outlining major environmental and economic drivers of the stock's distribution, life cycle, and fishery[cite: 53].

### Step 3: Analyze Ecosystem and Socioeconomic Indicators
[cite_start]Create, select, analyze, and monitor a specific suite of indicators representing the health of the stock and fishery[cite: 54]. [cite_start]We conduct statistical tests on trends between these indicators and direct stock metrics[cite: 55].

### Step 4: Communicate Ecosystem and Socioeconomic Considerations
[cite_start]The final step culminates in a stock-specific report and presentations that concisely convey the status of leading indicators to fisheries scientists and managers[cite: 56]. [cite_start]Indicators inform management decisions through four primary pathways[cite: 57]:
1. [cite_start]Providing context [cite: 58]
2. [cite_start]Supporting model assumptions [cite: 59]
3. [cite_start]Informing model parameters [cite: 60]
4. [cite_start]Providing model covariates [cite: 61]
