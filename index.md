## What is an Ecosystem and Socioeconomic profile?

Ecosystem and Socioeconomic Profiles (ESP) aim to address the critical need for more pathways to bring essential socio-ecological information into fisheries science and management.

ESPs are both a process and a product. The ESP process creates a traceable pathway for identifying and integrating key socio-ecological indicators into species-specific assessment and/or management decision making. The end result of the process is a succinct, focused report that distills information from a variety of sources and makes it more accessible, interpretable, and actionable for scientists and decision makers.

## Background on the Issue

Ecosystem-based science is a primary component of effective marine conservation and resource management, and NOAA Fisheries is committed to ecosystem-based management as our most powerful tool to maintain productive and resilient fisheries. This approach considers the entire ecosystem, including humans, to balance tradeoffs between ecological, social, and economic needs.

Our national standard guidelines require the consideration of ecosystem and socioeconomic processes with regard to specifying optimum yield. While progress has been made via comprehensive ecosystem status reports, a gap remains between conducting ecosystem research and integrating it within the fishery management process. Primary obstacles include a lack of a consistent approach to deciding *when* and *how* to test and incorporate this information.

## History of ESPs

The ESP was first conceived in 2014, building on NOAA Fisheries’ rich history of identifying ecosystem and socioeconomic pressures since the 1990s.

-   **2017:** The first ESP case study using Alaska sablefish was completed.
-   **2018–Present:** Developed and refined through the North Pacific Fisheries Management Council review process and recommendations from the Next Generation Stock Assessment Improvement Plan.
-   **Current:** ESPs are now being adapted and adopted across the nation with the help of a National ESP Coordination Team.
